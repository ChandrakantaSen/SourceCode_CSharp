Objective of the West Bengal Medical Council

       The Council is administered by a Committee of members constituted for  a   period  of  5  years  consisting  of  Registered  Medical Personnel nominated by Govt. of West Bengal, representatives of Medical Colleges of  the  State  and  the  members  elected by the General Members of the Council to attain the following objectives of the Council for the benefit of the General Public:
1.
To  register qualified  medical  graduates  required for practicing in the State  in order to prevent practice by unqualified persons. A register is maintained by this Council for the Registered Medical Practitioners.
Section 17
2.. To  watch  the  standard  of Training and Examinations in the Medical Colleges  & Hospital or the institutions already recognized or desirous to be recognized..
Section 19
3..
To  institute  enquiry on the allegation of patient parties for negligence, wrong   treatment, misbehavior  of   qualified  Medical  Practitioners registered with this Council and to decide & determine on the context. The  name of  the accused doctor, if found guilty of infamous conduct, on  enquire, is  removed from the register of medical practitioner, for a specific  period  as per decision of the Council. This name may further be  restored  after  expiry  of  penalty  period,  on  application  of  the doctor.
Section 25 (ii)
4.. To prevent practice by unqualified persons for safeguard and interest of public..
